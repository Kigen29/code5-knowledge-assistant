# Maintenance and Support Policy

## Purpose
Code 5 Developers provides maintenance and support for systems after launch. Maintenance keeps software secure, stable, compatible, documented, and aligned with changing business needs.

## Support Tiers
Support is classified as advisory support, standard maintenance, managed support, or critical operations support. The client agreement defines response targets, support hours, included services, escalation contacts, and reporting frequency.

## Ticket Intake
Support requests must be logged with requester, system name, environment, business impact, steps to reproduce, screenshots or logs, urgency, and affected users. Unclear requests should be clarified before engineering work begins.

## Severity Levels
Severity 1 means production is unavailable, data loss is suspected, or a security incident may be active. Severity 2 means a major workflow is degraded. Severity 3 means a normal defect or support request. Severity 4 means a low-priority improvement or question.

## Response Targets
Critical production incidents are acknowledged within 30 minutes during covered support hours. Severity 2 issues are acknowledged within 4 business hours. Severity 3 issues are acknowledged within 1 business day. Client contracts may define stricter targets.

## Maintenance Handover
Before a system enters maintenance, the delivery team must provide architecture notes, deployment process, known risks, monitoring details, backup and restore notes, credentials location, dependency list, and current support backlog.

## Routine Maintenance
Routine maintenance may include dependency updates, security patches, uptime checks, log review, backup verification, small bug fixes, and compatibility testing. High-risk maintenance must be scheduled and communicated before execution.
